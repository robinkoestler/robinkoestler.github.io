Work in Cryptography

Currently, I am a Ph.D. student in the Applied Crypto Group at the University of Luxembourg under the supervision of Jean-Sébastien Coron.

My research interests are in the area of cryptography, in particular in the design and implementation of cryptographic protocols and algorithms. I am currently working on fully homomorphic encryption (FHE), which allows to perform arbitrary computations on encrypted data. My goal is to improve the efficiency of the FHE bootstrapping, which is the pivotal procedure due to which FHE still remains not-so-much practical.

I did a survey on modern fully homomorphic encryption in my master's thesis, where I implemented and analysed the general bootstrapping approach presented in this paper (2021).

Current branch of work: Fast bootstrappings for CKKS

For CKKS, a FHE scheme for approximate computations, Jean-Sébastien Coron and I have published a method to improve the latency of the bootstrapping subroutine in the case of a small number of slots (i.e. the number of data slots for parallel computations). The paper (SPRU) is available here, and will soon be published. By using roots of unity, it constitutes a novel approach to a small family of existing bootstrapping equations, and together with the concurrent work of this paper (SHIP), it is among the most promising schemes towards practical FHE for Artificial Intelligence. My proof-of-concept implementation is available here. It is written in SageMath, which mixes Python and C++ (through the NTL library), and thus favors readability over performance. A more efficient open-source implementation by Jean-Sébastien Coron based on the OpenFHE library in C++ is available here. Our method has been also already improved by the work of Jean-Sébastien Coron and Tim Seuré, who have published the PaCo bootstrapping paper. As of May 2025, it remains exciting to see how the field of FHE will evolve in the next years. The figure on the right shows the performance of SPRU vs. CKKS depending on the number of slots.
